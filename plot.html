Plot

Things that should not have been forgotten

Many years ago in an earlier age, a great evil was banished from Arda by a fellowship of travelers chosen from all the races of free people. Since then, much about that evil has been forgotten, and names that were once feared or respected by even the bravest warriors or most skilled magicians have become mere fairy tales that are told to children. Some of the tales tell of times of fear to frighten young children into behaving, but the children's favorite stories tell of two friends. The friends were just simple people, nobodies really, and by all rights they shouldn't have been remembered if they had just lived out their lives like their neighbors. Fate, however, picked them for a much bigger role in history than they had bargained for.

Through the bravery of the two friends, Arda was ridden of a great evil, but a small part remained and fled to the north, nursing its wounds and biding its time. Over the past centuries, it has slowly kindled its power and is preparing to unleash it on an unsuspecting Arda. Already, rangers of the north are beginning to hear troubling rumors of a growing darkness.

The Ear of Corn

One rainy night, a messenger bearing news from the rangers has stopped by a roadside pub to rest for the night. It is clear that something is troubling him, and after having drunk an unseemly amount of beer, he begins to ramble about the rumors he's heard. As the night wore one, the rest of the pubs clientele gradually trickle out, until only the messenger and a few misfits remain.

Each of the misfits had a hard life behind them, and often came to the pub to seek some solace from the hard world and share a drink among what friends they had. Sensing a kindred spirit with the messenger, who is now maudlin and sobbing into his beer, they gather around his table and ask him about his troubles.
